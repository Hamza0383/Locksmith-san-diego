How Car Lockouts Happen and What to Do

Keys Left Inside With the Car Locked

This is the most common lockout situation we see. You set your keys on the seat or the center console, close the door, and it locks behind you. It happens in grocery store parking lots, at gas stations, outside restaurants — everywhere. It can happen to anyone. Don't feel bad about it. Just call us and we'll be there quickly.

Key Fob Battery Died and Backup Entry Failed

Modern cars rely heavily on key fobs. When the battery dies, the remote stop working. If you don't know about the manual key hidden inside the fob, you may feel completely stuck. Some cars also have backup entry options that are easy to miss. Our team knows how to handle fob-related lockouts for all makes and models. We'll get you in without any damage.

Child-Lock Activated and Door Won't Open

This one catches people off guard. You're outside the car and the door simply won't open from the outside. Child locks are designed to stop doors from opening from the inside — but they can also create confusion when you're on the outside trying to get in. Our technicians are familiar with this and many other car-specific quirks. We'll figure out what's going on and solve it fast.
