Types of Car Keys We Replace and Program

Transponder Keys With Chip Programming

Most cars made after the mid-1990s use transponder keys. These keys have a small chip inside. That chip sends a signal to your car's computer. If the signal doesn't match, the car won't start. We program transponder keys for a wide range of vehicles using professional equipment. The key is cut and the chip is coded to match your specific car.

Key Fobs and Proximity Smart Keys

Key fobs control your locks, alarm, trunk, and sometimes remote start. Losing one is a real problem. We replace and program key fobs for most vehicles on the spot. Proximity smart keys — the kind you keep in your pocket while the car detects you — are also within our range. We pair the new fob to your car and test every function before we leave.

Traditional Metal Keys and Switchblade Keys

Not every car uses a chip key. Older vehicles use a basic metal key that just needs to be cut correctly. We do this quickly and accurately. Switchblade keys — where the metal blade folds into the plastic body — are also common. These often combine a chip with a folding blade. We cut and program them as a single unit.
